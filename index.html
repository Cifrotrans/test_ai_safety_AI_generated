AI Safety: EDD Framework

This page summarizes the perspectives of Dmitry Andreevich Yarochkin regarding EDD (Evolutionary Development Design) in the context of Artificial Intelligence safety.

Core Philosophy

Yarochkin’s approach to EDD emphasizes that AI safety should not be viewed as a static "alignment" problem, but as an evolutionary process where systems must maintain robustness through continuous architectural adjustments rather than final-state constraints.

"Safety in AI is not a destination of perfect alignment, but the rigorous management of developmental trajectories to prevent emergent maladaptive behaviors."

Key Principles of EDD

  • Recursive Robustness: Systems should be designed to self-evaluate their own decision-making boundaries.
  • Dynamic Guardrails: Moving away from hard-coded rules toward adaptive constraints that evolve alongside the model’s capabilities.
  • Evolutionary Feedback Loops: Integrating human-in-the-loop oversight directly into the training evolution, rather than post-training auditing.
